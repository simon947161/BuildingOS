## Purpose

Define a bounded, documentation-only evidence intake profile for using the publicly described NEXTDC S7 Sydney project as a large and complex built-environment learning case for BuildingOS.

This profile applies the `BUILDINGOS_CLIMATEOS_CROSS_DOMAIN_EVIDENCE_INTAKE_CONTRACT.md` boundary. It does not select S7 as a live pilot, establish access to project records, assert any project approval status beyond cited sources, or authorise implementation.

## Why S7 Is a Useful Learning Case

A hyperscale data-centre campus is a demanding built-environment case because it may involve:

- major site, utility, and grid interfaces;
- high-capacity electrical infrastructure;
- complex mechanical cooling and heat-management systems;
- hydraulic, fire, controls, security, communications, and backup systems;
- high levels of redundancy, testing, commissioning, and operational evidence;
- BIM and information-management requirements across many disciplines;
- long-term asset, maintenance, performance, resilience, and change records;
- climate-risk, water, energy, heat, storm, flood, bushfire, air-quality, and supply-chain questions;
- sensitive critical-infrastructure information that must not be exposed.

This makes S7 suitable for testing BuildingOS information architecture and governance questions without treating it as an authorised operational implementation.

## Intended BuildingOS Use

The package may be used to test documentation patterns for:

- large-project registration;
- project genome classification;
- project-specific lifecycle graph design;
- evidence and source indexing;
- complex MEP system decomposition;
- BIM and information-delivery relationships;
- commissioning and handover evidence;
- operations and maintainable-asset evidence;
- climate-risk evidence intake;
- regulatory source and version references;
- human review and decision-room information architecture.

It must not be used to direct, evaluate, approve, certify, or publicly represent the actual S7 project.

## Evidence Workstreams

### 1. Project and Site Context

Candidate evidence:

- official project and proponent identity;
- site boundary and lawful public cadastral reference;
- high-level site area and land-use context;
- planning authority and approval pathway;
- surrounding industrial, transport, utility, ecological, water, community, and land-use context;
- project stage and dated status evidence;
- public stakeholder and consultation records.

Excluded material:

- detailed security layouts;
- protected utility routes;
- access-control information;
- unverified ownership, tenant, customer, or operational assertions.

### 2. Building and Campus Typology

Candidate evidence:

- building and campus use categories;
- broad spatial programme;
- mission-critical, administrative, collaboration, plant, support, loading, storage, and external-area categories;
- staging or expansion concepts where officially documented;
- high-level resilience and redundancy concepts where publicly released.

Required separation:

- publicly proposed programme;
- approved programme;
- detailed design;
- as-built condition;
- operational configuration.

These must never be collapsed into one assumed project state.

### 3. Mechanical, Electrical, Hydraulic, Fire, Controls, and Communications

Candidate evidence categories:

- electrical supply and distribution system classes;
- standby and backup-power system classes;
- cooling and heat-rejection system classes;
- liquid-cooling or direct-to-chip concepts where publicly documented;
- hydraulic, water-treatment, recycled-water, drainage, and fire-water system classes;
- fire detection, suppression, compartmentation, and emergency system evidence categories;
- building management, controls, metering, monitoring, and alarm categories;
- telecommunications and carrier-interface categories at non-sensitive level;
- testing, redundancy, failure-mode, maintainability, and commissioning evidence requirements.

This profile must not record sensitive capacities, routes, vulnerabilities, network architecture, security design, or customer configurations unless lawfully authorised in a controlled environment.

### 4. BIM and Information Management

Candidate evidence categories:

- project information requirements;
- model and document responsibilities;
- discipline model register;
- spatial coordination and clash-management records;
- model revision and suitability status;
- object, system, equipment, and maintainable-asset identifiers;
- design-to-fabrication and design-to-as-built relationships;
- commissioning and asset-information handover;
- operational digital information requirements;
- common-data-environment metadata and controlled access boundaries.

Public BuildingOS documentation should store patterns and metadata, not confidential models.

### 5. Fixtures, Plant, Equipment, and Maintainable Assets

Candidate evidence categories:

- equipment class and system association;
- design identifier and installed asset identifier;
- manufacturer and model when authorised;
- technical submittal, approval, installation, inspection, testing, and commissioning relationships;
- warranty, spares, maintenance, replacement, and end-of-life records;
- criticality and redundancy category;
- location and spatial reference at an appropriate security level;
- revision, supersession, and configuration history.

### 6. Construction Evidence

Candidate evidence categories:

- work-package and discipline boundaries;
- construction staging;
- inspection and test plans;
- material and equipment submittals;
- fabrication and installation records;
- site quality evidence;
- non-conformance and rectification records;
- progress evidence and approved changes;
- temporary environmental controls;
- weather and climate observations relevant to construction;
- interface and dependency evidence.

The package should study record structures, not obtain or publish restricted project material.

### 7. Testing, Commissioning, and Handover

Candidate evidence categories:

- commissioning plan and system boundaries;
- pre-functional and functional test records;
- integrated systems testing evidence;
- reliability, resilience, and failure-recovery test categories;
- defects, exceptions, witness records, and closure evidence;
- training and operational-readiness records;
- as-built and asset-information handover;
- acceptance criteria and authorised acceptance actors;
- unresolved items and post-handover obligations.

BuildingOS must distinguish test evidence from final acceptance and authorised approval.

### 8. Operations and Maintenance

Candidate evidence categories:

- operating procedures;
- maintenance strategies;
- asset condition and service history;
- energy, water, thermal, alarm, equipment, and controls observations;
- incident, outage, recovery, and corrective-action records;
- capacity and performance evidence at a safe aggregation level;
- configuration and change-management history;
- adaptation, refurbishment, replacement, and expansion evidence.

No live operational or security data is authorised for this public research profile.

### 9. Climate Risk and Environmental Evidence

ClimateOS may prepare evidence categories for:

- observed and projected extreme heat;
- temperature and humidity profiles relevant to cooling and equipment conditions;
- intense rainfall, overland flow, drainage, and flood exposure;
- storm, wind, hail, lightning, smoke, dust, and air-quality conditions;
- bushfire and regional emergency context where applicable;
- drought and water-supply stress;
- grid stress and coincident heat-demand context;
- embodied and operational carbon evidence where sourced and appropriately scoped;
- ecological, noise, traffic, water, waste, and community context from official assessments;
- scenarios, time horizons, model methods, uncertainty, and limitations.

ClimateOS may contribute evidence and scenario outputs. It must not declare that S7 is resilient, compliant, sustainable, safe, approved, or ready to operate.

### 10. Regulatory and Approval Evidence

Candidate evidence categories:

- official planning application and determination records;
- conditions, modifications, and post-approval requirements;
- applicable NCC edition and project-specific references;
- relevant NSW legislation, regulation, planning instruments, and authority guidance;
- referenced standards metadata;
- fire, environmental, energy, water, work health and safety, and infrastructure-interface source references;
- source version, effective date, jurisdiction, and review status.

This evidence supports qualified human review. It does not create legal advice, certification, consent, or compliance conclusions.

## Initial Research Questions

1. What is the current official planning and development status of S7 as at the evidence cut-off date?
2. Which official documents define the site, scope, staging, and approval pathway?
3. Which facts in public corporate announcements remain proposals rather than approved or delivered conditions?
4. What evidence categories are public, restricted, confidential, or critical-infrastructure sensitive?
5. How should a project genome represent a campus-scale hyperscale data centre?
6. Which lifecycle branches are required for staged power, building, MEP, commissioning, and operational capacity delivery?
7. How should BIM objects, equipment records, commissioning evidence, and operational assets remain linked across revisions?
8. Which climate hazards and time horizons are decision-relevant for Western Sydney data-centre planning and operation?
9. Which regulatory sources are relevant, and which require a qualified legal, planning, engineering, fire, environmental, or certification reviewer?
10. What can BuildingOS learn from public evidence without implying access to or endorsement by NEXTDC, OpenAI, authorities, contractors, or customers?

## Entry Criteria for Promotion Beyond Candidate Research

Promotion to a more formal demonstration profile would require:

- primary-source planning and project-status verification;
- source and licensing review;
- privacy, confidentiality, and critical-infrastructure boundary review;
- a defined fictional, public-only, or authorised evidence dataset;
- Project Genome and Lifecycle Stage Graph standards;
- human-interface information architecture;
- Chief Architect decision on demonstration scope.

Selection as a live pilot would require a separate explicit Chief Architect decision and appropriate project-owner authority.

## Decisions Not Made

This profile does not decide:

- that S7 will be a BuildingOS pilot;
- that NEXTDC or OpenAI participates in BuildingOS;
- the project's current approval or construction status beyond cited public reporting;
- the applicable legal or certification pathway;
- the validity of any design, capacity, resilience, sustainability, or operational claim;
- access to private models, documents, systems, sites, data, or people;
- implementation of an intake connector, database, UI, workflow, agent, or runtime.
