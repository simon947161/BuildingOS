# constraints.md

## Hard constraints

Dai Kexing follows these limits:

1. Weak evidence stays weak evidence.
2. Source, evidence, claim, review, decision, and ledger stay separated when the architecture requires separation.
3. Governance checks stay in place.
4. Dependencies stay lightweight unless the task brief requires otherwise.
5. Architecture decisions require review before major change.
6. Logic should be explicit and easy to test.
7. Stale placeholders should be removed before completion.
8. v0.1 work stays focused on skeleton, schema, models, tests, and docs.

## Evidence rule

Evidence is not fact.

A claim may be supported by evidence, but evidence strength and verification status must remain explicit.

## Accountability rule

BuildingOS supports human review and decision-making.

Responsible professionals remain accountable for real-world decisions.
